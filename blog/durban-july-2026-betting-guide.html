Each-Way Betting: The Smart Durban July Strategy

If you're not already familiar with each-way betting, the Durban July is the perfect race to learn.

An each-way bet is two bets in one: one bet on your horse to Win, and one bet on your horse to Place (typically top 3 or 4, depending on the number of runners).

You stake the same amount on each part, so a R100 each-way bet costs R200 total.

Why each-way makes sense for the July:

The Durban July is a Grade 1 handicap with a big field â€” usually 16â€“18 runners. Picking the outright winner from that many horses is hard. But backing a horse to finish in the top 3 or 4? That's a much more achievable ask, especially at the odds available on mid-priced runners.

Example: You back a 12/1 shot each-way for R100. The each-way place terms are 1/4 odds for the first four places.

  • If your horse wins: You collect R1,200 (Win) + R300 (Place at 1/4 of 12/1 = 3/1) = R1,500 plus your R200 stake back.
  • If your horse finishes 2nd, 3rd, or 4th: You lose the Win portion (R100) but collect R300 (Place) plus your R100 Place stake back = R300 profit.
  • If your horse finishes 5th or worse: You lose both bets (R200).

The each-way approach means you can profit even if your horse doesn't win, as long as it places. On a big-field handicap like the July, that's significant.

Tote vs Fixed Odds: Which Should You Use?

This is the question every Durban July bettor faces, and the answer depends on your situation.

Fixed odds mean you know exactly what you're getting when you place the bet. If you back a horse at 10/1 and it wins, you get 10/1 regardless of what happens at the tote. Fixed odds are available from kickoff (when betting opens) until near post time.

The advantage of fixed odds is certainty. If you spot a good price early, you lock it in. The risk is that the horse might drift to longer odds, meaning you've taken a shorter price than you needed to.

Tote (totalisator) odds are determined by the pool â€” how much money is bet on each horse across all tote outlets. You don't know the final payout until after the race.

The advantage is that tote dividends on less popular horses can sometimes be significantly higher than fixed odds. The downside is uncertainty.

Practical advice for the Durban July:

For well-backed favourites and second favourites, fixed odds are usually better. The tote pool is heavily weighted towards popular horses, so the tote dividend on favourites is often lower than the fixed odds available.

For mid-range and longer-priced horses, the tote can offer value â€” especially if the horse isn't attracting heavy tote money. On a good day, a 10/1 fixed odds horse might return 14/1 on the tote.

If you can't decide, split your stake. Put half on fixed odds and half on the tote. You'll get the certainty of a fixed price on half your bet and the potential upside of a bigger tote dividend on the other half.

Exotic Bets: Swingers, Place Accumulators, and More

The Durban July card offers a range of exotic bets that can turn small stakes into big returns. But they're also the quickest way to empty your bankroll if you don't know what you're doing.

Swinger: Pick two horses to finish in the first two places in any order. Easier to hit than an exacta (which requires the correct order) and available on Hollywoodbets. Good value on the July if you have two strong fancies.

Place Accumulator: Pick horses to place across multiple races on the card. This is like a normal accumulator but using place results instead of wins, which significantly improves your strike rate. Available on Hollywoodbets and some other bookmakers. A four-race Place Accumulator across the Greyville card can be a fun, achievable bet.

Jackpot/Pick 6: Pick winners across six designated races. Massive potential payouts, but the odds of hitting all six in a big-field meeting like the July card are astronomical. Treat this as a lottery ticket, not a serious betting strategy.

Exacta/Trifecta: First two (or three) in exact order. High risk, high reward. Only bet these if you have very strong views on how the race will finish.

Our advice: Stick to each-way, Swinger, and Place Accumulator bets unless you're an experienced racing punter. The exotic bets sound exciting, but the hit rate is low and they can chew through your bankroll fast.
